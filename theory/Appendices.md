$$P(Tv) = \phi\, P(v)$$

by equivariance. Therefore:
$$\phi\, P(v) = -\phi^{-1} P(v) \quad\Rightarrow\quad (\phi + \phi^{-1}) P(v) = 0$$

$$\ker P \supseteq V_{-\phi^{-1}}$$

$$P = L \circ P_\phi$$

$$S = c\,I_{V_\phi}$$

for some $c>0$, and we obtain:
$$L = U \circ c\,I_{V_\phi} = c\,U$$

Summarizing, any $P$ satisfying (i)–(iii) has the form:
$$P = L \circ P_\phi = c\,U \circ P_\phi$$

The scalar $c$ is fixed by the minimal-norm condition in (iii), and $U$ is an arbitrary orthogonal map on the target space, which corresponds to a choice of orthonormal basis in the image.
